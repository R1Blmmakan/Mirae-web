SAMBOOSA

A traditional Middle Eastern snack shaped like a triangle, similar to a samosa, and very popular in Gulf countries such as the United Arab Emirates, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait. This dish is made from thin pastry sheets filled with various ingredients, such as seasoned minced meat, chicken, cheese, or vegetables. It is then folded into a triangle shape and deep-fried until crispy.
Samboosa has historical roots in ancient trade routes that brought South Asian culinary influences to the Arab region, and it later developed with local flavors that are lighter but still rich in spices.

TASTE

The taste of Samboosa is savory with light and delicious spices.

TEXTURE

The texture of Samboosa is crispy on the outside with a soft filling inside.

CALORIES

The total calories of Samboosa range from about 1,600–1,700 kcal. The ingredients include ground meat (about 890 kcal), potatoes (about 230 kcal), onions (about 70 kcal), wheat flour (about 364 kcal), lemon and thyme (about 10 kcal), plain yogurt (about 60 kcal), and spices (about 15 kcal). This food is rich in energy because it contains protein, carbohydrates, and fats that provide a savory and satisfying taste.

INGREDIENTS:

DOUGH:
  • 360g medium-protein wheat flour
  • 200ml Water
  • 3g Salt
  • ½ Lemon
FILLING:
  • 350g Ground Beef
  • 2 Potatoes
  • 1 Onion
  • 3 Cloves Garlic
  • 2g Grated Ginger
  • 11g Curry Powder
  • 10g Cumin Powder
  • 6g Coriander Powder
  • 6g Salt
  • 6g Black Pepper
  • Coriander Leaves and Green Onions to taste
LEMON DILL YOGHURT SAUCE
  • 100g Yoghurt
  • 3ml Lemon Juice
  • 2g Thyme
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper

HOW TO MAKE:

DOUGH:
  • Mix the flour, salt, and lemon juice, then stir well. Add water little by little so the dough is not too wet. If it becomes too wet, add more flour while mixing.
  • Knead the dough until smooth, then let it rest for about 15–20 minutes. Make sure it is covered with a cloth or plastic. Then divide it into balls of 30–45 grams. Roll each dough ball thinly into a round shape.
  • Stack three layers of the rolled dough with a light brush of oil between them (do not use too much). Roll the three layers again until slightly thinner (do not press the edges too hard so they are easy to separate).
  • Toast the three layers of dough together over very low heat for only a few seconds, then flip immediately (do not wait until the pan is too hot). Place them on a tray.
  • Separate each layer carefully (do not tear them), shape them into perfect circles, then cut each circle into two halves.
  • From one half-circle, fold it into a triangle shape, fill it with samboosa filling until compact, then seal it. Use a mixture of flour and water as glue to close the open side.
  • Fry the samboosa over medium heat until golden brown, then remove and drain.
FILLING:
  • Dice 2 potatoes into small cubes and boil them until slightly soft. Dice 2 onions and mince 3 cloves of garlic.
  • Sauté the onion and garlic with butter until fragrant, then add 2 tablespoons of water so the garlic does not burn. Add 350 g of ground meat and cook until it turns brown. Keep stirring so the meat cooks evenly.
  • Add seasoning such as salt, black pepper, cumin powder, mushroom broth powder, curry powder, and a little sugar. Taste to adjust the flavor.
  • While stirring, add a little grated ginger gradually so the flavor is not too strong.
LEMON DILL YOGHURT SAUCE:
  • Add 100g Yoghurt
  • Add 3ml Lemon Juice
  • Add 2g Thyme
  • Add Seasonings (Salt, Black Pepper)
  • Stir until well combined and taste
