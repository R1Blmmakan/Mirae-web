CHICKEN MACHBOOS

A traditional chicken and rice dish originating from the Arabian Gulf region and considered one of the national foods of the United Arab Emirates. It is also popular in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Qatar. This dish developed from ancient spice trade routes that connected the Middle East with India and Persia, creating a unique blend of basmati rice, chicken, and aromatic spices such as cardamom, cinnamon, turmeric, cumin, and loomi (dried black lime), which gives the dish a savory flavor with a slight tangy taste

TASTE

It has a balanced sweet and sour taste, where the distinctive sweetness of the dates appears first, followed by a slightly tangy yogurt aftertaste.

TEXTURE

It has a creamy and smooth texture from the yogurt, combined with a slightly fibrous and chewy texture when you bite into the dates.

INGREDIENTS:

DOUGH:
  • 20 g oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 15 g chopped ginger
  • 10 cloves garlic
  • 3 green chilies
  • 30 g garam masala
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 cardamom pods
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 star anise
  • 6 cloves
  • 15 g black pepper
  • 1 kg whole chicken thighs
  • 15 g salt
  • 15 g ground coriander
  • 4 g turmeric powder
  • Water as needed
  • 30 g chicken stock
  • 1 dried lemon
  • 1 green chili
  • 2 tomatoes, cut into quarters
  • 1 kg basmati rice
  • 45 g butter
  • 60 g saffron water
  • 15 g melted butter
  • 15 g garam masala
  • 15 g Arabic spice powder / curry powder
SAUCE DAQOOS:
  • 4 medium red tomatoes (300–400 g), diced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 30 g tomato purée
  • 3 green chilies
  • 5 g coriander leaves
  • 27 g olive oil
  • 100 ml water
  • 2 g cumin powder
  • 7 g salt
  • 6 g black pepper
  • 15 g lemon juice

HOW TO MAKE:

  • Add 20 g of oil to the pan.
  • Sauté the diced onion, then add 15 g chopped ginger, whole garlic cloves, and 2–3 green bird’s eye chilies. Sauté until lightly browned.
  • Then add:
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4–5 cardamom pods
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 6 cloves
  • 15 g black pepper
  • Add 1 kg chicken and sauté for 2 minutes. Then add 15 g salt, 15 g ground coriander, and ½ tbsp turmeric powder. Stir well until evenly mixed.
  • Add enough water, then add 30 g instant chicken stock, 1 dried lemon, green chili, and 4 pieces of tomato. Cook for about 30 minutes until fragrant and fully cooked.
TOPPING:
  • Sauté 15 diced shallots until they turn brown and fragrant.
  • Prepare another pan, add 30 g of oil, then sauté 10 g of raisins.
  • Add the raisins to the sautéed shallots, stir briefly, then add 5 g of salt and 10 g of cardamom powder.
CHICKEN SEASONING:
  • Prepare saffron water, then add 45 g ghee, 20 g garam masala, and Arabic spices. Mix well. Pour the mixture over the chicken and let it rest for 5 minutes.
  • Then fry or roast the chicken until fully cooked.
SAUCE DAQOOS:
  • Blend the Tomatoes: Blend the tomatoes until smooth. You can also finely chop them if you prefer a slightly chunky texture.
  • Sauté the Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a small pot. Add the minced garlic and chili, sauté until fragrant and slightly browned.
  • Cook the Tomato Paste: Add the tomato paste to the sautéed garlic, stir well, and cook for about 1 minute to remove the raw taste.
  • Cook the Sauce: Pour the blended tomatoes into the pot. Add water, cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  • Simmer: Let it come to a boil, then reduce the heat. Cook until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate (about 5–10 minutes).
  • Finishing: Add chopped coriander leaves and lemon juice or vinegar. Stir briefly and turn off the heat.
  • Serve: Serve warm or cold as a dipping sauce for Arabian rice.
RICE:
  • Remove the chicken that was previously braised, then strain out the herbs. Prepare the biryani rice that has been soaked for 30 minutes. Add the biryani rice to the pot, add salt to taste, then add 45 g butter. Stir the rice gently and cook for about 5 minutes or until cooked, then add 60 g saffron water.
  • During the 5 minutes of cooking, gently stir the biryani rice.
